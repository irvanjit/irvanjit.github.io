procedure

 

Construction

  1. 1.To begin, obtain all of the necessary equipment, tools, materials and components required for construction

  2. 2.Before beginning construction, a USB cable is needed with a 35 ohm resistor attached to safely test all of your LEDs. Start by taking your USB cable and stripping off the female type-b (square) end of the cable, and the plastic coating closer to the end of that cable using wire strippers. The red and black wires are all that are required. Strip off the coating on these wires as well, and after entwining a resistor to one of the wires, and if needed, extending these wires with more of the same colour and soldering everything into place. Electrical tape can be used to hold everything together.

  3. 3.Use this cable to test each of your LEDs by plugging the male end into a USB port of a powered computer, and connecting the negativ end of the cable (black) to the cathode (shorted lead), and the positive end (red) to the anode (longer lead). The LED should turn on. Make sure all of the LEDs are functional.

  4. 4.Bend the cathodes on each of the LEDs into an even L-shape, such that the end of the lead is perpendicular to the bulb and pointing away from the anode.

  5. 5.Use wire cutters to cut out 64 pieces of the steel wire, each approx. 20cm in length.

  6. 6.In order to solder the LEDs onto these wires to construct the rows of the cube, it may help to tape down the wire that is being worked on and then soldering each LED to it.

  7. 7.Each row will have 4 LEDs soldered to it. Position the LEDs so that each anode has just the tip (0.5cm) crossing the wire and the cathodes are well apart from the wire. Use a ruler to make sure that each LED is approx. 4cm apart and relatively centred on the wire. It is made easier if the wire crosses overtop of the anodes, then the other way around.

  8. 8.Solder each of the LEDs, making sure that the joints are well-rounded and will hold the LED in place.

  9. 9.Repeat this process until 16 rows of LEDs have been constructed.

  10. 10.Position 4 rows, using tape so that they are parallel, aligned and 4cm apart.

  11. 11.Position 4 more wires so that each is crossing 4 cathodes with the same distance as the anodes, or enough to ensure even length of the level (the interconnected rows). Make sure that each LED is positioned 4cm apart in all direction from the others. Again it would help to use masking tape throughout this entire process, and to have the wires crossing overtop of the cathodes.

  12. 12.Solder each of the cathodes to the wires, making sure to create solid, conductive joints.

  13. 13.Throughout this entire process, the LEDs should be constantly tested to ensure conductivity of the wires and joints, as well as the conditions of the LEDs. Use the cable, and instead of connecting the wires to each LED, connect the positive end and the negative to each cathode, then to each wire connecting the cathodes, making sure that all of the LEDs light.

  14. 14.Repeat this process until 4 complete levels (4 x 4) have been made. There should be ample wire sticking out on the ends of these levels. For now, leave this as it is.

  15. 15.The next process is to construct the actual structure of the cube. Begin by taking the drywall protectors, and cutting out 8 pieces that are 16cm long, and 4 pieces that are 18cm long. Use strong scissors to do this.

  16. 16.Cut out 6 pieces of plexiglass, 4 of them 16x18cm, and two of them 16x!6cm. Use a permanent market to mark out the cut lines. Use a utility knife to make the cuts. Use a ruler to graze the lines from both sides several times, and then snapping the unwanted pieces off by placing the plexiglass at the edge of a table, and snapping the two parts apart.

  17. 17.Use this process to cut out all of the required pieces.

  18. 18.Take two of the longer 16x18 pieces, and mark anode holes in each of them. Position them such that there are 16 holes in each piece, 4cm apart in all directions from each other, leaving 3cm on either side of the longer end, and 2cm from the top and bottom (shorter side).

  19. 19.Drill each of these holes, for a total of 32 holes in both of the plexiglass sheets.

  20. 20.Place the sheets with holes to the side for now. Take another 16x18 piece, and 4 frame pieces, two of them 16cm and two 18cm.

  21. 21.If the plexiglass sheets have a protective coating on them, they should be removed immediately before adding the sheets to the frame.

  22. 22.Take of the plastic backing from these 4 frame pieces, and use a glue gun to add onto the already sticky coating on the frame pieces, attaching these pieces appropriate to the plexiglass such that one of the flat sides of the frames would stick to the outer edges of the sheet, and the other would be perpendicular to it. Use enough glue to create a strong bond between the plexiglass and the frame edges.

  23. 23.Add 4 more frame edges to the corners of the now constructed base. The structure is now ready for the two plexiglass sheets to be added. Add these pieces to the appropriate sides of the cube (longer side).

  24. 24.Add four more frame edges of appropriate length to attach the two sides together from all sides.

  25. 25.Take the levels constructed previously and bend the cathodes so that they are as far apart from the anodes as possible.

  26. 26.Insert the ends of the anode wires for each level into the drilled holes, such that the four levels are laid one ontop of another, 4cm apart from each other.

  27. 27.Centre each of the levels and bend the ends protruding outside of the cube structure so that it is pointing downwards along the outer surface of the cube.

  28. 28.The cathodes will not require any support as there is ample support for the level from the anodes.

  29. 29.Cut 4 pieces from the CAT5e wire, each approx. 0.75m, and strip the ends of these wires so that for each piece, one side would have approx. 4cm exposed, and the other approx. 18cm exposed.

  30. 30.Separate all of the stranded wires, and strip off 5mm of coating from each end of the wires

  31. 31.Use the schematic to wire the anode and cathode rows to the cube, using electrical tape to keep the cables organized, and using consistent colour-coding to make the wiring process easier.

  32. 32.The next process is to construct and wire the breadboard. At this point, the schematic is the primary tool used as a reference to wire this board. First insert all of the ICs in the appropriate spot, leaving 3-2 pins on either side of the breadboard. When wiring, the 5mm should be exposed, and an appropriate length should be kept so that none of the pins are block from access. Follow the schematic explicitly, placing all components and wires where needed.

  33. 33.Add the other ends of the CAT5e cables, again, explicitly following the schematic to wire the cube.

  34. 34.Once all of the CAT5e wires have been attached, the remaining plexiglass sheets required for the cube can be added to the structure, using a glue gun.

  35. 35.The construction of the cube is now complete.

The procedure for constructing this cube is laid out into two different sections. The first is for the actual construction of the cube, and the second is for the initial testing and programming.


Please note that the method described below is one way of going about the construction of this cube. There are multiple ways that the construction can be approached, however, the method described here will result in a cube has a very strong and solid structure which will ultimately be easier to manage.


Lastly, the Photos and Videos sections serve as excellent companions for this method, and it is highly recommended to refer to the videos throughout this entire process, as they offer an extremely detailed depiction of the cube.

testing and programming

  1. 1.Before beginning, a serial cable is required. Refer to the schematic to solder wires to the DB9 pin. The wires need to be exposed on either side by 5mm. Three wires need to be soldered to the pins; it is preferable to use three different colours.

  2. 2.Once the wires are soldered. connect them as it is shown in the schematic. Also refer to the PICAXE manual, under the section for 28X1, for details on the pins.

  3. 3.Insert the USB cable male end into a powered computer, and insert the serial pin into a DB9 serial port on the same computer.

  4. 4.On the opposite end of the cable, the resistor that has been attached needs to be removed to provide sufficient power to the display. Once this is done, insert the ends of the cable in the appropriate locations, (positive to positive, negative to negative).

  5. 5.Open the PICAXE programming editor and refer to the PICAXE programming manual to program the cube to test the basic function of the circuit and all of the LEDs. For more information, refer to the Program Code section of this lab report. Once the program has been typed in, check syntax and then program the display using the buttons available in the toolbar.

  6. 6.The cube should now be successfully programmed, and the serial cable can be removed from the entire system. USB or battery power is required to display an image onto the cube.

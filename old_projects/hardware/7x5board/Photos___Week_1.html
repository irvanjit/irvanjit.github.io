A regular Soldering Iron, used for making permanent liquid iron joints on copper plate coated circuit board

The layout of the circuit board, before any of the components were soldered.

Beginning to solder the 13 pin connector onto the soldering board, be very careful while soldering these, they are very difficult to solder due to their small size.

Starting to put in the 5th row of the LEDs onto the board, before soldering it. The transistors and resistors have already been soldered.

While soldering resistors, be careful not to burn the copper plate, as that would cause it to not get a strong hold in the circuit board.

To solder a component on the circuit board, keep soldering iron at least 6-7 inches away from the face, and make sure the soldering wire is touching the iron for it to release liquid metal.

Another image of soldering the resistors, make sure your hand is at least 2 inches away from the soldering iron

The finished circuit board, complete with all soldering, transistors, resistors, 13 pin wire connector, 35 LEDs and ready to be wired!
